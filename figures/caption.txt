Caption: Distribution of CCEI scores in two simulated samples (10,000 individuals). Preferences follow a CES utility over allocations to self and other, with parameters drawn uniformly from empirically plausible ranges ($\alpha \sim U[0.5,1]$, $\rho \sim U[-0.5,0.95]$). Choices are perturbed along the budget line by mean-zero Gaussian noise centered at the optimum; noise severity is governed by its standard deviation.The top panel uses the Andreoni and Miller (2002) budget set, while the bottom panel uses the optimized budget set. The two simulations are matched at the individual level: each individual has identical utility parameters and the same noise realization across the two budget sets.
